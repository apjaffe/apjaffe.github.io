Jewish Customs

Some aspects of a Jewish wedding may be unfamiliar to those who haven't previously attended one. Below you can find a booklet that defines many of the terms and describes each step in detail.

A few aspects that are particularly notable:

  • For seven days up until the wedding, the bride and groom do not see each other (or even directly communicate). All events prior to the Chuppah ceremony will be held separately for either the bride or groom, but not both.
  • On the day of the wedding, the bride and groom fast from sunrise until Chuppah.
  • On the wedding day and seven days after, special prayers are said at the end of each meal in honor of the bride and groom. This is called Sheva Brachot, and is said to be especially auspicious for those who are also looking for their soulmate!
